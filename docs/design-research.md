# Design research

This review was conducted against the official pages on July 10, 2026. Live DOM, interaction, and official project descriptions were used; some image-heavy/WebGL pages did not yield reliable automated screenshots, so the conclusions focus on interaction and composition rather than copied pixels. These references are principles, not asset or layout sources.

## 1. Universal Everything - Future You

Source: [Future You](https://www.universaleverything.com/art/future-you)

- The body-tracking work is dormant without a visitor, then makes physical effort visibly consequential; the participant and synthetic form create a relationship for one bounded encounter.
- It begins as a primitive form and develops many possible variations, making growth over time legible without pretending to reproduce the visitor's appearance.
- Its collapse when time expires gives the temporary relationship a clear ending instead of letting the interaction trail off.

**Use:** direct embodied causality, primitive-to-complex growth, and a meaningful dissolve/reset. **Avoid:** humanoid anatomy, robotic-avatar mimicry, and language suggesting the system has learned a person's identity.

## 2. Google Creative Lab - Move Mirror

Source: [Move Mirror](https://experiments.withgoogle.com/move-mirror)

- The proposition is understandable in one sentence: enable a webcam, move, and see a real-time response. Pose technology stays behind an immediate physical loop.
- Instructions and feedback make a technically complex browser experiment approachable without exposing a diagnostics dashboard.
- The original live experiment is inactive, but its official page and linked source preserve a useful pattern: explain the input, request it at the point of use, and reward the first motion quickly.

**Use:** low-friction consent, clear positioning guidance, and immediate confirmation that movement matters. **Avoid:** literal pose matching, image-database retrieval, or making one remote model/load failure block the artwork.

## 3. Memo Akten - Learning to See: Interactive

Source: [Learning to See: Interactive](https://www.memo.tv/works/learning-to-see-interactive/)

- Visitors manipulate ordinary material under a camera and watch a neural system reinterpret it in real time; visible transformation makes computation tangible.
- Switching among differently trained networks exposes that machine perception is conditional, not a neutral or certain account of reality.
- The same premise supports a brief playful encounter or sustained careful exploration, giving the work depth without a long tutorial.

**Use:** frame the portrait as an interpretation, expose change through the artwork, and leave room for ambiguity and discovery. **Avoid:** AI-as-truth framing, confidence theater, diagnoses, or copying its generated landscapes.

## 4. Patatap

Source: [Patatap](https://patatap.com/)

- A single input produces synchronized sound and motion immediately; no explanatory layer separates action from consequence.
- A small, consistent interaction vocabulary is easy at first contact while combinations create longer-term expressive depth.
- Its explicit flashing-image warning is a useful reminder that audiovisual immediacy can create accessibility risk when intensity is uncontrolled.

**Use:** tight event-to-sound-to-motion timing, simple first contact, and depth through combination. **Avoid:** its shapes or palette, rapid flashing, mandatory sound, or turning the portrait session into a keyboard toy.

## 5. Species in Pieces

Source: [Species in Pieces](https://species-in-pieces.com/)

Live verification limitation: the official URL returned `ERR_SSL_PROTOCOL_ERROR`, so no fresh screenshot could be captured. The observations below are deliberately limited to the official indexed project text and the supplied research brief rather than presented as a current pixel-level audit.

- One disciplined geometric grammar can create many related but distinct silhouettes; consistency makes state changes feel authored rather than preset-driven.
- Morphing transitions can carry conceptual continuity: the viewer sees one structure becoming another instead of unrelated scenes replacing each other.
- Restrained navigation leaves the central specimen memorable and keeps controls subordinate to the artwork.

**Use:** one coherent mark-making language, silhouette-level distinction, and transitions that preserve ancestry. **Avoid:** animals, copied polygon compositions, or a carousel of disconnected portrait presets.

## 6. Bruno Simon

Source: [Bruno Simon's portfolio](https://bruno-simon.com/)

- Navigation is embodied: moving through the world is how visitors discover content, and small secrets reward continued exploration.
- Audio, quality, renderer, device-specific controls, respawn, and reset are explicit, so playfulness is supported by practical recovery and adaptation.
- The portfolio remains understandable when optional online systems are unavailable, demonstrating that spectacle should not make the core experience fragile.

**Use:** discoverable details, explicit quality/mute controls, device-aware input, and graceful recovery. **Avoid:** vehicles, game objectives, 3D-world navigation, achievements, or a control scheme that competes with the portrait.

## 7. Warhol Art

Source: [Warhol Arts](https://warhol-arts.webflow.io/)

- Oversized editorial typography, short labels, and generous shifts in scale establish exhibition pacing before explanatory prose appears.
- Artwork and title carry the hierarchy while navigation behaves like marginal notation rather than a persistent product toolbar.
- Transitions join poster-like moments into a sequence, allowing information to arrive around the art without boxing it into a dashboard.

**Use:** exhibition-poster first viewport, editorial rhythm, sparse labels, and receding chrome. **Avoid:** copied Pop Art branding, novelty typography everywhere, crowded exhibition navigation, or a long theatrical loader.

## 8. Active Theory

Source: [Active Theory](https://activetheory.net/)

- Full-canvas compositions treat the viewport as a scene rather than a webpage with an effect placed behind it.
- Cinematic transitions and interaction-led pacing connect project states into a narrative; rendering technique serves the scene change.
- High-impact visuals are paired with responsive input and performance awareness, so immersion depends on timing and adaptation as much as fidelity.

**Use:** edge-to-edge composition, state-led cinematic transitions, and quality-aware visual craft. **Avoid:** WebGL for its own sake, ornamental post-processing, opaque navigation, or effects that conceal slow input response.

## Synthesis for Pattern of One

### Principles we will use

- A full-canvas, single-route encounter in which one uncertain seed wakes, responds, remembers, reconciles, and dissolves.
- Immediate physical/audio causality followed by richer behavior that emerges through repetition and personal-baseline change.
- One restrained visual grammar: luminous core, filament traces, pulse rings, negative space, and persistent motifs rendered in one session accent.
- Sparse editorial framing; controls appear at the edges only when needed and never turn live signals into a dashboard.
- Clear permission language, fast fallback paths, device-specific quality, reduced motion, recovery controls, and deterministic demo profiles.
- Ambiguous but traceable interpretation: final observations describe measured within-session changes, never identity, emotion, or personality.

### Principles we will deliberately avoid

- Literal bodies, faces, avatars, animals, pose mirroring, and figurative particle silhouettes. A face-like concept image supplied outside the repository is explicitly rejected as the visual direction.
- Copied palettes, polygons, shapes, worlds, navigation models, motion signatures, artwork, branding, or assets from any reference.
- Game mechanics, galleries of presets, dashboards, decorative AI imagery, gratuitous WebGL, constant particle noise, and sound-dependent comprehension.
- False machine certainty, diagnostic labels, personality claims, or any implication that the portrait is a true or permanent self.
